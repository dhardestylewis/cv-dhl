### Post 1
Three years ago, we released TabPFNv1 – the first tabular foundation model – and, well... let's say the reception was mixed.

Back then, many in ML told us neural networks couldn't seriously compete on tabular data. One prominent voice even tweeted: "Neural nets for tabular is the ML equivalent of wearing socks with sandals." Ouch.

Fast forward to 2026, and the narrative has flipped. Just this week, Forbes published "From Text To Tables: Why Structured Data Is AI's Next $600 Billion Frontier", highlighting the massive opportunity we've been building toward at Prior Labs.

Tabular data is catching up to language models: a bit later, but with huge potential gains in accuracy, speed (seconds vs. hours/days of tuning), flexibility (zero-shot on messy real-world data), easier deployment, and far less operational overhead. No more fragile task-specific pipelines.

We're proud to have pioneered this with TabPFN (now at v2.5, scaling to much larger datasets while keeping the core advantages), and we're just getting started on the next generation of multimodal tabular foundation models.

### Post 4
I'm incredibly excited to see our latest research "Accurate predictions on small data with a tabular foundation model" published in Nature Magazine - representing a major milestone for foundation models in tabular data (https://lnkd.in/eB8KR3eX)

Tabular data is everywhere: clinical trials, financial models, environmental monitoring, and manufacturing processes. Yet, while AI models for text and images dominate the headlines, tables have been left behind. Why? Because tabular data is messy, diverse, and highly contextual.

The question guiding our research: What discoveries are already hidden in our databases and spreadsheets? Projects like the UK Biobank, supported by billions of dollars in investment, have collected incredible amounts of clinical and demographic information. Unlocking the patterns within these datasets could help solve some of the greatest challenges in science and healthcare, from predicting patient outcomes to designing personalized treatments.

Two years ago our work on TabPFN (https://lnkd.in/e_GStC9g) demonstrated something remarkable: a neural network, pre-trained on millions of synthetic tasks could learn fundamental patterns that generalize across problems. While surprising, v1 worked only with small classification tasks and numerical features—limiting its real-world application.

After 2 years of intensive research, we're proud to introduce TabPFN v2.

Our model has been trained on 130 million synthetic datasets, learning to understand fundamental patterns in tabular data. On small datasets, in less than 10 seconds, TabPFN outperforms an ensemble of the strongest baselines tuned for 4 hours. As a generative transformer-based foundation model, this model also allows fine-tuning, data generation, density estimation, and learning reusable embeddings.
